# **2. AI in Robotics**

## **2.1 Key Challenges**

Robotics aims to:

* Assist or replace humans in:

  * Hazardous environments
  * Repetitive tasks

### Applications:

* Exploration and surveillance
* Surgical robots
* Companion robots
* Autonomous vehicles
* Intelligent transportation systems

---

## **2.2 What is a Robot?**

### **Automaton**

* Executes predefined tasks
* Works in a fixed environment
* Limited adaptability

---

### **Autonomous Robot**

* Adapts to its environment
* Uses a continuous loop:

👉 **Perception → Decision → Action**

---

### Origin of the term “Robot”

* Comes from Czech word **“robota”** (forced labor)
* Introduced by Karel Čapek (1921)

---

## **2.3 From Automata to Robots**

### Historical Evolution:

* Antiquity: mechanical/hydraulic automata
* Middle Ages–18th century: mechanical clocks, animated figures
* 20th century:

  * Electronics
  * Microelectronics → modern robotics

Example:

* Jacques de Vaucanson (18th century)

---

## **2.4 Increasing Mechanical Complexity**

Examples:

* **Unimate**

  * First industrial robot (1961, 5 DOF)

* **Honda ASIMO P3**

  * Advanced humanoid (28 DOF)

---

## **2.5 What is Intelligence in Robotics?**

A robot’s intelligence involves:

* Building **world representations**
* **Adapting** to constraints (learning)
* **Reacting in real time**
* Understanding/expressing **social signals**
* **Cooperating** with other agents

---

## **2.6 AI Approaches in Robotics**

### **Symbolic AI**

* Logic-based reasoning
* Planning algorithms
* Probabilistic models

---

### **Machine Learning**

* Neural networks
* Deep Reinforcement Learning
* Bio-inspired methods

---

### Key Insight:

There is **no universal solution**:

* Methods must match the **problem constraints**
* Robotics faces:

  * Limited computation
  * Noisy sensor data

# **3. Types of Robots**

## **3.1 Mobile Robots (Wheeled)**

Examples:

* **TurtleBot** (research/education)
* **WiFiBot**
* **Mirokai**

👉 Used for:

* Navigation
* Human interaction
* Research

---

## **3.2 Legged Robots (Bio-inspired)**

* Quadrupeds & hexapods
* Inspired by animals

Examples:

* **ANYmal C**
* Snake-like locomotion robots

👉 Advantage:

* Better mobility in rough terrain

---

## **3.3 Humanoid Robots (Bipeds)**

Examples:

* **ASIMO**
* **NAO**

### Challenges:

* Balance
* Walking
* Climbing stairs

### Why humanoids?

* Designed for **human environments**

---

## **3.4 Advanced Bipeds**

Example:

* **Boston Dynamics**

👉 Focus:

* Dynamic motion
* Robust locomotion

---

## **3.5 Autonomous Vehicles**

Example:

* **Google Self-Driving Car Lexus RX450h**

Technologies:

* LiDAR (laser sensors)
* GPS
* AI perception

---

## **3.6 Aerial Robots (UAVs)**

Types:

* Fixed-wing drones
* Multirotors

Example:

* **PX4 Vision**

👉 Key challenge:

* Planning and control in **3D space**

---

## **3.7 Soft & Modular Robots**

Example:

* Octopus-inspired robots

👉 Characteristics:

* Flexible materials
* Adaptable structures

# **4. Current Trends & Challenges**

## **4.1 Major Advances**

Driven by:

* AI
* Electronics
* Mechatronics

### Progress in:

* Locomotion
* Perception
* Decision-making

---

## **4.2 Open Challenges**

### Mixed or limited success in:

* Human-robot interaction (companion robots)
* Navigation in extreme environments
* Multi-robot coordination

---

### Emerging areas:

* Swarm robotics
* Micro/nano robots
* Soft robotics

---

# **5. Key Takeaways**

* Robotics combines:

  * Mechanics
  * Electronics
  * Artificial Intelligence

* Core loop:
   **Perception → Decision → Action**

* No single AI approach fits all problems

* Rapid progress, but many **open challenges remain**
